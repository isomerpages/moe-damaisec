<p><strong>Racial Harmony Day Mini Carnival</strong>
</p>
<p>This year marked a special milestone for Damai Secondary as we held our
first-ever Racial Harmony Day Mini Carnival. The celebrations kicked off
with the <em>What Say You?</em> assembly programme, which encouraged students
to reflect on what harmony means to them. Following that, everyone was
treated to a nostalgic spread of old-school snacks and they enjoyed traditional
games such as the eraser battle and hopscotch, bringing a sense of <em>kampung</em> spirit
to our school grounds.</p>
<p>However, the commemoration did not stop there. Throughout the week, activities
continued to deepen our appreciation of Singaporeâ€™s multicultural identity.
During CCE lessons, students explored themes of multiculturalism and the
importance of respect in a diverse society. As part of their National Education
learning journey, Secondary 1 students visited various places of worship,
gaining first-hand insight into the beliefs and practices of different
communities.</p>
<p>Our Racial Harmony Day experience this year was not just a one-day celebration.
It was a meaningful journey of learning, reflection and shared experiences
that truly lived out the spirit of harmony.</p>
